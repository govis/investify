# Growth in Global Electrification and the Critical Minerals Deficit
Image: Thesis 2.jpg

### Executive Summary

The global transition toward a decarbonized, highly electrified economy has initiated a commodity supercycle characterized by severe, mathematically unresolvable supply constraints over the medium term. The simultaneous scaling of electric vehicles (EVs), residential and commercial heat pumps, grid expansion, and the aforementioned AI data centers requires exponential increases in the consumption of critical transition metals.7 Copper, the foundational element of global electrification, faces a projected structural deficit of 10 million metric tons by 2040\.8 Concurrently, the emerging hydrogen economy is establishing a powerful new demand vector for Platinum Group Metals (PGMs), shifting market balances into structural deficits and establishing a compelling long-term thesis for critical mineral producers capable of navigating complex geopolitical supply chains.9
