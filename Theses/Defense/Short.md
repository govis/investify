# Impact on the Defense Industry as a Result of Geopolitical Tensions and Conflict

### Executive Summary

The post-Cold War era of unipolarity and frictionless global trade has officially concluded, replaced by a highly volatile "Multipolar World" characterized by great-power rivalry, regional kinetic conflicts, and the rapid weaponization of economic supply chains.3 The escalation of the Israel-Iran conflict in 2026, the protracted war in Ukraine, and the U.S.-led political intervention in Venezuela have fundamentally altered national security doctrines worldwide.22 Governments are enacting massive, structural increases in sovereign defense spending while simultaneously executing multi-trillion-dollar industrial policies to reshore critical manufacturing and secure independent energy supplies.3 The nature of modern warfare has also shifted, creating explosive demand for advanced, AI-enabled counter-measures, precision munitions, and autonomous systems.26
