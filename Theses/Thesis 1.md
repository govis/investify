# Rapid Advance in Artificial Intelligence and the Infrastructure Supercycle
Image: Thesis 1.jpg

### Executive Summary

The rapid commercialization of Generative AI and the relentless pursuit of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) have triggered an unprecedented capital expenditure (CapEx) cycle among hyperscale technology companies. Projected to exceed $600 billion in 2026, this spending is fundamentally transforming the technology sector from a cash-rich, asset-light model into a highly leveraged, physical infrastructure-dependent industry.4 Concurrently, the immense energy and thermal management requirements of AI data centers are straining global power grids, shifting the primary bottleneck of AI advancement from semiconductor availability to electricity generation and advanced liquid cooling deployment.4 The market is witnessing a divergence between the infrastructure providers—who are realizing immediate, massive revenue growth—and the foundational model builders, who face a highly debated return on investment (ROI) and mounting debt obligations.4
